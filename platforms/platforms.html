Popular Fantasy Platforms

There are many choices when it comes to fantasy platforms, and they all have different features. Almost all of them are free and perform similar functions. I've included a few of the ones I've used and my opinions on each. Whichever one you choose, spend some time getting used to way it works for easier transactions in the season. And, if you're the commissioner of a new league, it's even more important to get to know the platform intimately.

All logos are property of their respective business.


ESPN

ESPN is one of the largest platforms available and used by a very large amount of fantasy leagues. The website and app are free to use, and they offer several other fantasy sports that can all be linked to one account. This makes it incredibly easy and intuitive if a user wanted to play fantasy baseball, hockey, etc. on one platform.

I think ESPN is a good option for people that may be new to fantasy and are running a simple scoring format.

Sleeper

Sleeper is a newer, free platform that is quickly growing in popularity. It's got an impressive amount of customization options to tailor a league exactly to what's wanted. Sleeper is a great choice for more complex scoring or dynasty leagues. However, it is still very user-friendly if a simpler league wanted to use that option.

Sleeper also offers easy ways to do sports betting in the app if it's legal in your state.

Yahoo

Yahoo is one of the oldest platforms that was pivotal when mainstream fantasy football switched to the internet. However, it's still extremely popular and powerful as a fantasy platform. Many of the longest running leagues still run on Yahoo.

CBS

CBS is another platform that is very powerful for customizing your league to your specific needs. Some players don't particularly find the app user-friendly, but many others refuse to use anything but CBS.
