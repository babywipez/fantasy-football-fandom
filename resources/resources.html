Resources

In this day and age, there's literally thousands of fantasy football websites, tv shows, podcasts, and more. It can actually be a little overwhelming if you don't know where to start. The FF Fandom team is here to help!

FantasyPros

FantasyPros is one of the best organizations in the industry for providing resources. Most of their content is free, but they do have a paid membership to unlock more robust and powerful tools. Their rankings are extremely helpful, and they have a very knowledgeable team that works on them.

FantasyPros also hosts a free mock draft simulator where you can input your league's settings and do a mock draft against their industry experts. Mock drafts are a great way to practice drafting fantasy teams with different strategies and team composition. You can even invite other people into one of your mock drafts to join you!

Other Helpful Links

Podcasts are a great way to keep up with NFL news, injuries, ask start/sit questions, etc. Some of my personal favorites are The Fantasy Footballers, Fantasy Football Today, and FantasyPros.

CBS HQ is a fantastic resource if you have access to the CBS TV channel or an internet connection. The fantasy portion of their show airs every Sunday morning before kickoff.

ESPN has a huge amount of fantasy advice and knowledge across their various channels. The Youtube channel has a catalogue of videos that are extremely helpful.

NFL on NBC is another great resource. The main analyst, Matthew Berry, is one of the legends of the industry and a fantastic mind for fantasy insight. Their Youtube Channel
