What Is Fantasy Football?

Fantasy football traces its roots back to the early 1960s and has been rapidly gaining popularity since. The idea of fantasy football was created in 1962 by Wilfred "Bill" Winkenbach, an Oakland, California businessman and limited partner in the Oakland Raiders. He gathered with 7 other football enthusiasts in a New York hotel room and created what would be the very first fantasy football league, along with its rules. The rest, as they say, is history.

The premise of fantasy football is that a group of people will each simulate owning their own football team. The players in the league will either all gather at a predetermined location & time, or they can meet virtually, and they "draft" their team using players that are active in the NFL.

The draft can operate in a variety of ways (order, number of rounds, etc.), but the basic idea is to mimic the roster of a NFL team. That usually means each team would need a Quarterback, 1 or more Running Backs, 1 or more Wide Receivers, 1 or more Tight Ends, a Kicker, and a Defense. Once the draft is complete, the teams will play against each other each week of the NFL season using the players they drafted. At the end of the season, a champion is crowned.

Fantasy football has changed substantially over the years. When Winkenbach's league was playing, every stat, score, and point had to be tracked using paper and pen. Now, there are hundreds of websites and apps that make it easier and more convenient than ever to bring the game to the user.

Some Valuable Terms

Like most hobbies, fantasy football includes its own unique words and phrases. If you're unfamiliar with the hobby, these can initially sound like gibberish. I've included a few popular terms that fantasy players often use.

PPR
Point Per Reception - this means that a player scores 1 point every time they catch a pass. Some leagues may also award half a point per reception (Half PPR) or other variations.
FLEX
These positions are used as a flexible way of putting different NFL positions into one roster spot. For example, you can usually play either a wide receiver or running back in a FLEX roster spot.
D/ST
Defense / Special Teams - a Defense and Special Teams unit will score points based on how well they played against the opposing offense that week.
Dynasty League
This type of league is unique in that every team keeps their players going into each following year. There is still a draft, but only the incoming rookies are drafted.
Waiver Wire
This refers to the pool of players that are not currently on a fantasy roster. You can add these players to your team within certain timeframes and drop underperforming players to the waiver wire.

Fantasy Football In 2023

In this era of sports, it's hard to turn on a tv, podcast, or any media source without hearing about sports betting. It's wildly popular and controversial, especially in the United States. Many people may be hesitant to try fantasy football because of this. While many fantasy leagues do a "buy-in" (an initial amount of money that each player must contribute into a prize pool prior to the draft), many do not. There are a variety of ways to enjoy fantasy football, and most of them don't actually revolve around making money.
